Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? We've got answers. Find everything you need to know below.

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of "driving" your bike—not just "riding" your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider. Always wear a helmet and protective gear.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Check your helmet for any cracks, dents, or signs of wear. If it has been in a crash, replace it immediately. Most manufacturers recommend replacing helmets every 3-5 years, or sooner if you notice any damage or if the padding has become worn out.

My bike has been in storage, is it safe to ride?

Before riding a bike that has been in storage, check the tires for pressure and condition, test the brakes, check the chain for rust or stiffness, and ensure all bolts are tight. It's recommended to have a professional mechanic inspect the bike if it's been stored for more than 6 months.

What documents do I need to purchase a bike?

You'll need a valid driving license, proof of identity (national ID or passport), proof of address, and passport-sized photographs. For financing options, additional documents like income proof and bank statements may be required.

Do you offer financing options?

Yes! We offer flexible financing options with competitive interest rates. You can choose from various EMI plans ranging from 12 to 60 months. Contact our sales team for more details about available financing options.
